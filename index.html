Computer Graphics Research Map (Interactive)

Background. Computer graphics began with Ivan Sutherland’s Sketchpad (1963), which proved that humans could draw and manipulate geometry interactively on a CRT. As memory became cheaper in the 1970s, raster framebuffers replaced vector displays, enabling shaded images and texture mapping. By the 1980s–90s, global illumination (ray tracing, radiosity) and dedicated hardware (SGI, early GPUs) industrialized 3D. The 2000s introduced programmable shaders and physically based rendering across film and games. Since 2020, neural methods (NeRFs, diffusion, denoisers) have fused graphics with AI, accelerating and expanding the pipeline.
